01 Why this project is being done
Business Problem
License requests processed through a manual, time-consuming workflow
Takes up to 7 business days and 5 hours of effort per user
Delays prevent planners from starting work and reduce customer satisfaction
Why This Matters
4 new users per week require access or access changes on average
Current processing costs the organization $372/week in labor
Manual steps make the process hard to scale, transfer, and audit
Project Goal
Optimize the Kinaxis license access process for supply planners by implementing automation, an approval workflow integration, and centralized documentation — improving customer experience and reducing lead time from 7 to 4 business days.

04 Current status — September 1, 2026
Risk #4 hit: workflow actions not synchronized
During the August 31 pilot, a requester reported that an automated request submitted about a week earlier still had no update. The team reviewed the automation log and found that the approver's decision had been made, but the workflow did not capture it correctly — so the requester was never notified and the license was never granted.
Mitigation already in place before the risk hit
The team had already planned to test and validate the workflow before and during the pilot phase — as documented in the Risk Management Plan: "Conduct workflow testing and validation to ensure approval decisions flow correctly." This enabled the team to quickly identify the exact failure point in the automation log.
Contingency response
Automated workflow paused until the issue could be corrected
Requests being processed manually so license access continues without delay
Team working with IT to fix the workflow logic — estimated to take more than 15 days
Affected pilot user notified directly and license manually granted
Project impact
Launch milestone delayed — new date to be determined after IT identifies and resolves the bug
Additional time and IT resources needed for troubleshooting, correction, and retesting

05 Frequently asked questions
How will user data be managed and protected?
All license request data will be stored in a cloud-based centralized repository hosted on AWS. Access is controlled through AWS IAM (Identity and Access Management), which restricts repository access to specific authorized team members only — including the COE License Processor, Kinaxis Admin, Request Approver, Technical Manager, Audit Expert, and Regulatory/Finance team.
When will the project be completed?
The final milestone (user satisfaction survey) is now planned for January 15, 2027, revised from December 15, 2026 due to the workflow issue identified during the pilot phase. The go-live launch date is to be determined after the IT fix is completed and retested.
How will this project benefit the company?
Supply planners will receive access in 4 business days instead of 7. Processing effort per user drops from 5 hours to 30 minutes, and the company can expect to save $5,000 per week by reducing delays. Total cost per user will drop from $2,951 to $1,701 — a 42% reduction. The automated workflow also reduces human error and maintains a complete, audit-ready record of every access decision.
